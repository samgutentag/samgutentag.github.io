Buckeyes

Formatted

Prep Time: 20 minutes Cook Time: 1 hour Difficulty: Medium Source: Halfbakedharvest.com

Ingredients

4 cups roasted peanuts, unsalted preferred

1/4 cup honey

3 tablespoons softened butter, to your taste

1 tsp vanilla extract

2 cups chopped dark chocolate, melted

Directions

Prepare Peanut Butter by placing 4 cups peanuts into Vitamix container. Use app to blend nut butters. Or follow instructions on link.

In the bowl of a stand mixer, combine the peanut butter, honey, butter, and vanilla and beat until smooth and creamy. The mix will be runny.

Spread mixture onto a sheet pan lined with parchment paper, aim for 1/2" thick layer.

Place in the freezer for 20 minutes.

After 20 minutes, remove from the freezer and divide into roughly 1" squares with a bench scraper or knife.

Roll pieces into balls between your hands and return to freezer for at least 45 minutes, being sure they are very cold before coating in chocolate.

Tempering Chocolate with Sous Vide

After 20 minutes prepare tempered chocolate in Sous Vide. Start by vacuum sealing chocolate into a bag. Seal is most important part, any water at all will ruin melted chocolate.

Bring water bath to 115°F and insert chocolate bag and allow to melt completely. After 5 minutes check bag and massage in your hands to ensure all pieces are melted. Do not exceed 7 minutes total. Chocolate should melt quickly.

Lower temperature setting to 81°F, goal is to get water to this temperature as quickly as possible but with control. Add ice or transfer out hot water with cold tap water in 2 cup intervals until you reach desired temperature.

Once it hits 81°F, set temperature to 90°F.

It will take roughly 5 minutes to get to 90°F, massage the bag every minute or so while coming up to temperature. Once you reach 90°F the chocolate can stay there indefinitely or until ready to use.

Stick a toothpick into the top of each ball. Working with one ball at a time, dip the frozen balls into the chocolate leaving a small opening at the top so the peanut butter can peek out.

Place the balls back on the baking sheet. Repeat with the remaining balls.

Store in the fridge until ready to eat!
