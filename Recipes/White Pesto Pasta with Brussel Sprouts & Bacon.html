White Pesto Pasta with Brussel Sprouts & Bacon

Formatted

Servings: 6 Source: bonappetit.com

Ingredients

For the Pesto:

½ cup walnuts

Kosher salt

4 oz fresh ricotta

1 lemon, zest

2 garlic clove, finely grated

2 tsp oregano, finely shopped

2 oz finely grated Parmesan

¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil


Everything Else:

1 lb tube shaped pasta, rigatoni or spaghetti

40 Brussels sprouts

1 lb thick cut bacon

Kosher salt

Pepper

Directions

Pesto

Preheat oven to 350°. Toast walnuts on a rimmed baking sheet, tossing once, until golden brown, 6–8 minutes.

Let cool, then finely chop or process to a powder in a food processor.

Vigorously mix walnuts, ricotta, lemon zest, garlic, oregano, and Parmesan in a large bowl until well combined.

Add oil 1 Tbsp. or so at a time, stirring well after each addition, until all the oil is incorporated.

Season pesto with salt and pepper.

Divide into containers in thirds, store extra servings the refrigerator.


Pasta

Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil.

Fry the bacon in a skillet over medium-high heat until crispy.

Take the bacon out of the pan (set aside until needed) and leave the grease in there.

Add the Brussels sprouts to the pan, stir them until they're nicely coated in the bacon grease, and cover the pan with a lid.

Reduce the heat to medium-low. Cook for 5 minutes.

Add the bacon back into the pan. Cook, uncovered, for a few more minutes or until the Brussels sprouts are as tender and/or browned as you want.

You can cover the pan again if you wish (especially if you want the Brussels to be super soft); just keep an eye on them so they don't scorch.

Season with salt & pepper if needed.

Cook pasta in pot of boiling water, stirring occasionally, until al dente.


Plating

Scoop ¼ cup per 2 servings of pasta cooking liquid into bowl with pesto and stir gently to combine (it’s okay if some of the pesto is still in small clumps).

Using a spider, tongs, or slotted spoon, transfer half of pasta directly to bowl with pesto.

Stir to coat then add remaining pasta and stir to coat again.

Mix energetically until pesto thickens enough to lightly coat pasta, about 2 minutes; the pasta should have sauce clinging to it but there should also be some extra sauce that collects in bottom of bowl.

Divide pasta among bowls and serve with more Parmesan alongside for sprinkling over.

Top with Brussels sprouts and bacon, or add to mixing bowl and toss before plating.

Notes

Recipe makes enough pesto for 6 servings, 3 nights of dinner for two. Set aside pesto to be used on each day and do not combine with pasta water until ready to serve.
