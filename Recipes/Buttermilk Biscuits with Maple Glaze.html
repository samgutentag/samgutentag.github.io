Buttermilk Biscuits with Maple Glaze

Formatted

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 30 minutes Servings: 9 Source: cravingsbychrissyteigen.com

Ingredients

3 Tbsp heavy cream

1½ Tbsp maple syrup

½ tsp fine sea salt


½ cup (1 stick) very cold unsalted butter

500g flour

1 tsp sugar

1 Tbsp baking powder

1 tsp fine sea salt

½ tsp baking soda

1½ cups buttermilk, or Whole Milk and 1 Lemon

Directions

Preheat the oven to 425°F.

Make the Maple Cream

Combine the cream, maple syrup, and salt in a bowl and reserve.

Make the Biscuits

If you can't find buttermilk, mix the juice of 1 Lemon into the Milk, set aside.

Pulse flour, sugar, baking powder, sea salt, and baking soda in the food processor until incorporated, 10 pulses.

Pulse in the butter until some pea-sized pieces remain, 15-20 pulses.

Transfer the mixture to a big bowl and stir in the buttermilk until everything in the bowl is just moistened

Transfer the dough to a floured surface, knead the dough 3-4 times with floured hands, then pat into a 8-x-8-x-1-inch-high rectangle.

Cut into three equal-sized long strips, then cut those each into three pieces so you have 9 squares.

Arrange the biscuits close together (like almost kissing) on a big baking sheet, and brush with some of the maple cream.

Bake until golden, 20 minutes (or 17 minutes in a convection oven), brushing with maple cream again 1 minute before removing.

Remove from the oven and serve with the remaining maple cream for dipping.
