Sous Vide Carnitas Tacos

Formatted

Prep Time: 30 min Cook Time: 18 hr Servings: 6 Source: Recipes.anovaculinary.com

Ingredients

2 pounds pork shoulder or country-style pork ribs, cut into 2-inch strips, when in doubt get more


½ white onion, peeled and quartered

½ white onion, diced for serving

1 Tbsp lime juice

2 tsp kosher salt

1 tsp cumin

1 tsp dried oregano


2 bay leaves

1/2 orange, zested and quartered


12 Small corn tortillas, for serving

Fresh cilantro leaves, for serving


1 bunch radishes, thin slices or matchsticks

2 limes

2 cups Rice

1 cup shredded Mexican cheese


Green Pepper Cholula Sauce, for serving

2 Avocados, for serving

Directions

If prepping in advance

Dice half white onion, store in container in refrigerator until ready to serve.

Matchstick radishes, combine with juice of half a lime and 1/2 tsp kosher salt, toss or shake container and let rest overnight in the refrigerator.

Trim fat from pork and pat dry with paper towels.

Season pork with salt, cumin and oregano

Combine pork, quartered onion, lime juice, orange zest and bay leaf in a large zipper lock or vacuum seal bag.

Juice the orange into the bag and add spent rind to the bag

Seal the bag using the water immersion technique or a vacuum sealer on the moist setting.

Night before

Set the Anova Sous Vide Precision Cooker to 185ºF (85ºC).

Place the bag in the water bath and set the timer for 8 hours. (Or up to 16 hours)

Cover the water bath with plastic wrap to minimize water evaporation.

Once cook is complete

When the timer goes off, remove the bag from the water bath.

Pork should be tender enough to shred by hand or with a fork if too hot, don't spread too finely, looking for larger chunks to broil

Note: Pork can be stored at this stage in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

Heat the broiler to high and line a rimmed baking sheet with aluminum foil.

Spread pork evenly on baking sheet, season to taste with salt.

Broil pork, stirring occasionally, until browned and crisp on all sides, 3 to 5 minutes.

Heat tortillas over an open flame until charred.

Serve pork on tortillas with toppings of choice.
