Honey Butter Chicken Biscuit

Formatted

Servings: 8 Source: Joshua Weissman

Ingredients

2 cups buttermilk

1/2 Tbsp gochujang

2 tsp white pepper

1 Tbsp garlic powder

1 Tbsp onion powder

1.5 tsp salt

1 Tbsp smoked paprika


375 g all-purpose flour

2 Tbsp baking powder

1 tsp fine sea salt

1 tsp sugar

140 g cold butter

1.25 cup buttermilk


2 cups all-purpose flour

1 tbsp salt

1 tsp smoked paprika

1 Tbsp garlic powder

1 Tbsp Serrano powder, optional

1 1/2 tsp fresh ground black pepper


1/2 cup wildflower honey

1 Tbsp avocado honey optional

6 sage leaves

3.5 Tbsp really good salted butter


4 chicken breasts, cut in half for sandwiches

Directions

Marinade

To a large bowl add buttermilk, gochujang, white pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, salt and smoked paprika.

Whisk to Combine.

Cut chicken breast in half, these are the chicken patties.

Lay between two pieces of plastic wrap and pound all pieces to even thickness, aim for 1/2 thick-ish.

Add chicken to marinade and cover with plastic wrap, let marinate 1 hour at room temperature or overnight in the refrigerator.


Buttermilk Biscuit

Preheat over to 425F

In a food processor combine all purpose flour, baking powder, fine sea salt. Blitz a few time to combine.

Cube butter into 1/2 inch cubes and place in the freezer for 5-10 minutes, we need the butter as cold as possible without being frozen.

Add to food processor and pulse until pea sized clumps are formed. This can also be done with a fork or biscuit cutter.

Transfer to a large bowl if needed and then slowly stream in buttermilk to mix and form a dough.

Dump dough onto a lightly floured work surface and knead gently until it comes together.

Laminate the dough by rolling into a rectangle, folding towards the middle in thirds like a letter, rolling out again and repeating the letter fold.

Cover in plastic wrap and let rest in the refrigerator 5 minutes.

Roll into a rectangle about 3/4" thick and use a 3-4 inch biscuit cutter to cut as many as possible.

Scraps can be rolled back out and cut again.

Place on a parchment lined baking sheet, lightly brush the tops with buttermilk

Place into over set to 425F for 15-17 minutes or until tops are golden brown.


Dredge

Fill a heavy bottomed pot with frying oil until about 2.5-3 inches deep, no more than 3/4 of the way full.

Set over the stove and bring oil up to 350F.

In a medium bowl combine all purpose flour, salt, smoked paprika, garlic powder, serrano powder and black pepper, whisk to combine.

To bread chicken pull piece out of marinade, drop into flour mixture, toss and press aggressively to cover completely. Chicken should be completely dry. Set each piece aside.

Lay chicken into the oil in batches 2-3 pieces at a time at most for 5-7 minutes or until golden brown.

Set on a wire rack set over a baking sheet to drain and rest.

Honey Butter

In a small pot combine honey, avocado honey and whisk at medium heat.

As soon as it becomes hot turn off the heat and add sage leaves.

Begin whisking and add butter. Do not stop whisking until fully emulsified.
