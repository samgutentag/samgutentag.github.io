Bacon and Cheese Waffles

Formatted

Servings: 10 Source: bonappetit.com

Ingredients

430 g flour

1 tbsp sugar

2 tsp baking powder

1 tsp baking soda

1 tsp kosher salt

3 egg whites

3 egg yolks

1/2 cup melted butter

1 cup whole milk

1/2 lemon, juiced

3/4 cup soda water

Non-stick vegetable oil spray

1 cup crumbled Bacon

3/4 cup shredded sharp white cheddar, rustic cut

Maple syrup

Directions

Cut bacon into roughly 1/2 inch pieces, then cook over medium high heat until crispy.

Set aside to try on a paper towel lined plate.

Preheat oven to 240°F and plug in waffle iron (or turn on) to start getting it hot.

Ideally, sift flour when weighing.

Whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and kosher salt in a large bowl.

Using an electric mixer, beat egg whites in a medium bowl until medium-soft peaks form.

Whisk egg yolks, melted butter, buttermilk, and soda water in a medium bowl; gradually whisk into dry ingredients.

Fold in egg whites.

Do not over mix, it will looks like way too much egg white for the first 6-7 folds. Aim for no more than 12 fold total. (These are just numbers though, fold as few times as possible to disperse in the batter)

Coat waffle iron with non-stick vegetable oil spray.

Pour batter onto iron, spreading it into corners (amount of batter needed will vary according to machine).

Scatter 1 rounded Tbsp. ham and 1 Tbsp. white cheddar over each waffle.

Cook until golden brown and cooked through, about 4 minutes.

Transfer to a baking sheet; keep waffles warm in oven between batches. Serve with butter and warm maple syrup.
