Breakfast Burrito

Formatted

Difficulty: Easy Servings: 12

Ingredients

24 eggs

1 white onion, diced

2 bell peppers, any color, diced

6 garlic cloves, crushed

4 jalapeño, diced

2 cups shredded Mexican cheese


1 lb breakfast sausage

1 lb bacon

12 flour burrito tortillas

4 cups frozen hashbrowns


aluminum foil

Directions

In a large skillet, cook bacon strips until crispy. Set aside and then cut into roughly 1/4” pieces.

Add Breakfast Sausage to pan and cook over medium high heat, breaking into small pieces as you go.

Crush Garlic over pan when about 2 minutes remain, stir to combine well with Breakfast Sausage.

Once Breakfast Sausage is cooked through, remove from skillet and set aside.

Drain excess grease from pan and add onion, peppers, and jalapeños. Season with Salt and toss to coat with grease.

Saute Jalapeños, Onion, and Bell Peppers over medium high heat until softened, about 10 minutes. Set aside.

Cook hash browns according to package instructions, crispier the better, and stir consistently to create large chunks instead of a single large hash brown.

Once cooked, remove about half from skillet.

Add half of the back in Bacon, Sausage and Vegetables back to the pan, stir to combine.

Make a well in the center of the skillet for the eggs and add Vegetable oil to cover the exposed skillet.

In a large bowl crack and which half of the Eggs, pour into the skillet.

Let the eggs set slightly on the bottom then stir to create a soft scramble.

When roughly 2/3 of the eggs are set, mix with the remaining ingredients in the pan and add half of the shredded cheese to combine.

Once cooked, remove from pan and set on a large baking sheet too cool.

Repeat egg cook and mixing process with remaining half of ingredients.

Let mixture cool completely before wrapping burritos, wrapping hot mixture results in "sweaty" wet burritos.

Soft tortillas wrap easier, place each tortilla on the hot skillet for about 5-10 seconds per side to soften before filling.

Spread mixture and wrap burritos in foil.

Notes

To Reheat and Eat: Move Burrito from freezer to fridge about a day before. Melt about 1 Tbsp of Ghee in a skillet over medium-low heat until melted and then add burrito to pan. Roll to coat with ghee.

Cover and let burrito come up to temperature for about 4 minutes. Remove cover and roll burrito to continue browning on each side.

To Reheat from Freezer: Preheat Oven to 400 and bake frozen burrito on center rack for 45 minutes or until hot all the way through.
