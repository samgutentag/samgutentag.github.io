Breakfast Burrito

Difficulty: Easy Servings: 12 Source: artofmanliness.com

Ingredients

18 eggs

1 white onion, diced

2 green bell peppers, diced

3 garlic cloves, crushed

2 cups shredded cheese

1 Tbsp Milk

2 lb breakfast sausage

1 lb bacon, bits

12 flour burrito tortillas

4 cups frozen hashbrowns

4 medium sized jalapeños

Directions

Cut bacon into roughly 1/2 inch pieces and cook in a Large Skillet over medium high heat until cooked through.

Crush Garlic over pan when about 3 minutes remain, stir to combine well.

Once finished, set aside and let cool.

Remove excess bacon grease from skillet and brown breakfast sausage, breaking into dice sized pieces as it cooks. Set aside and let cool.

Saute Jalapeños, Onion, and Bell Peppers over medium high heat until softened, about 10 minutes. Set aside and let cool.

Cook hash browns according to package instructions, crispier the better, and stir consistently to create large chunks instead of a single large hash brown.

Once ready, add back all cooled ingredients and mix well to combine.

In a large bowl crab and whisk eggs together with milk and cheese.

Adjust skillet to high heat and add eggs, mixing to combine, we want large dense curds.

Once eggs are cooked, spread out on a large cookie sheet to let mixture cool completely before wrapping burritos, wrapping hot mixture results in "sweaty" wet burritos,

Roll 2-4 tortillas loosely in a paper towel and microwave about 14 seconds to soften.

Spread mixture and wrap burritos in foil. Will keep in freezer for up to 3 weeks.

To Reheat and Eat: Move Burrito from freezer to fridge about a day before, then microwave on 80% power roughly 45 seconds.
