Brined Pan Roasted Pork Tenderloin with Herb Pan Sauce

Prep Time: 4 hours Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4 Source: Joy of Cooking, page 489, 546, 1046

Ingredients

3 Tbsp honey

3 bay leaves

2 fresh rosemary springs

4 fresh thyme sprigs

6 garlic cloves, crushed

1 Tbsp black peppercorns

1/2 cup kosher salt

4 cups water


1-1.5 lb pork tenderloin

1 Tbsp bacon fat, lard or vegetable oil


1/2 cup shallot, minced

2/3 cup dry white wine

1 Tbsp lemon juice

4 tsp Dijon Mustard

1 bay leaf

1/2 cup heavy cream

1 1/2 tsp butter

2 Tbsp parsley, tarragon, thyme, or combination, minced

Directions

Prepare Brine by bringing half of the water to a boil with the bay leaves, rosemary springs, thyme springs, garlic cloves, peppercorns, salt and honey, whisking to dissolve the salt and honey.

Transfer the brine to another container big enough to hold the pork and add the remaining water.

Cool completely before adding the pork. You can put the container over ice or in the fridge to cool it faster.

Place the pork in the brine and refrigerate for 2 to 4 hours.

Thoroughly rinse and pat dry with paper towels. Then let the pork sit out for 30 minutes to get to room temp before cooking

Preheat oven to 300F

In an oven safe pan or skillet, melt Bacon fat over medium high heat.

Brown pork tenderloin well on all sides, roughly 6-8 minutes.

Transfer pan to middle rack of oven and roast uncovered until thermometer reads 145 at thickest part, about 15-20 minutes.

Transfer to plate and let stand, tented with foil 5-10 minutes before slicing.

Meanwhile, prepare Herb Pan Sauce
