Pasta Dough

★★★★

Formatted

Servings: About 1 pound Source: food.com

Ingredients

2 cups semolina flour

2 cups all-purpose flour

2 eggs

1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil

1/2 tsp salt

1 cup cold water

Directions

Prep the Dough

Combine flour and salt in a large bow, whisk to mix together and then sift into another bowl.

In a small bowl, whisk eggs then add olive oil and water, whisk to combine.

Add egg mixture to stand mixer bowl and pour in half of the flour mixture.

Using the flat beater, turn mixer to speed 2 to combine for 20 seconds.

Add remaining flour mixture and set to speed 2 for 20 seconds.

Switch to dough hook and knead on speed 2 for 2-3 minutes until a dough ball has formed.

Remove dough from bowl and hand-knead for 2 to 3 minutes.

NOTE: Good pasta dough should be elastic and pliable, but FIRM (not soft like bread dough). It should not stick to your fingers or fall apart.

To test for the correct consistency, pinch a small amount of dough together after kneading for 2 to 3 minutes -- if the dough stays together without sticking to your fingers or falling apart, it should work well.

If too soft, add more flour by dusting the top of the dough and knead some more, continuing to dust the dough with flour until achieving the right consistency.

If too dry, wet your hands and knead some more, continuing to wet your hands a little at a time & knead until the right consistency.

Wrap dough in plastic wrap and put in the refrigerator for a minimum of 1 hour (this step is important).

Remove dough from refrigerator and let it rest for 15 minutes. Using your hands, roll dough into a log, about 12 inches long.

Cut log into 8 slices, then flatten each piece slightly. Spread slices out so they aren't touching and cover with plastic wrap.

Make the Noodles

Using the widest setting turn mixer to speed 2 and taking one piece of the flattened dough, feed through rollers. Fold dough in half & roll again. Repeat 3 more times, lightly dusting the sheet of pasta in between each rolling.

Move adjustment knob to setting 2 and feed the dough sheet through the rollers once.

Move adjustment knob to setting 3 and feed the dough sheet through the rollers once.

Continue to increase roller setting until desired dough thickness is reached, (4 or 5 for fettuccini noodles)

IMPORTANT: Flour the pasta rack or the plastic hangers before placing pasta on them.
ALSO, separate the strips of pasta as best you can, so they aren't touching.

After cutting each sheet, hang to dry on a pasta rack. Dry for a minimum of 10 minutes.
