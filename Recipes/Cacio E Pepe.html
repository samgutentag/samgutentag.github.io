Cacio E Pepe

Source: Justkitchin.com

Ingredients

Pasta of your choice - Spaghetti or bucatini is traditional

Pecorino Romano cheese - Splurge a little, buy a good quality wedge, and grate it yourself

Freshly ground black pepper

Butter

Extra virgin olive oil

Directions

Bring a pot of salted water to boil. A pinch of salt per cup of water is probably fine. You want a pretty low water-to-pasta ratio to maximize “starchiness”, so definitely don’t go filling the entire pot.

Finely grate a bleep-ton of Pecorino Romano cheese. You really can’t have too much. It can be expensive, though… so use your judgement.

Add your pasta to the pot.

Add cheese into a heat-safe bowl that fits snugly over your pot of pasta water. Mix in a few splashes of starchy water a little at a time - too much at once will cause the cheese to seize up… Your goal is to form sort of a cheese paste. Also mix in black pepper, a drizzle of olive oil, and a few pads of butter.

When the pasta is cooked, transfer it into the bowl with the cheesey-paste. Keep the hot pasta water simmering on the stove. Place the bowl with the pasta on top of said pot, forming a double boiler. Be sure that the bottom of the bowl is not in contact with the liquid water. Mix with a rubber spatula and occasionally scrape down the sides of the bowl to prevent stickage.

Adjust, adjust, adjust. If the sauce is too thin, add more cheese and allow the heat to work its magic. If the sauce is too thick/nonexistent, add more pasta water. Season to taste.

Warm up your serving bowl - this makes a huge difference, as it keeps the sauce nice and warm and creamy. A cold serving bowl will cause some unwanted thickening, and your sauce may disappear entirely. Lastly, I find that the best time to serve is when the sauce is on the thinner side… As it sits in the serving bowl, it will continue cooking and tighten up significantly. You want to try to compensate for this.

Shower with more Pecorino and a few cracks of black pepper. Take a picture, show your friends, and give yourself a pat on the back. You are a pasta master. Eat and

enjoy.
